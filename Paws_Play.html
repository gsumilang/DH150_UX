About

Paws // Play is a social networking app centered on people and their pets.

While studies have shown that pets play a significant role in our overall well-being, they also affect our social lives in more ways than we may think. From self-proclaimed "animal lovers" to extreme opinions about who is a "dog person" versus a "cat person", the relationship that we have with our pets holds a stake in our social identity.

Whereas pets have earned a rightful place in our social media sphere - from pet Instagram accounts to popular Internet memes - there appear to be no social media apps that place pets and their owners at the forefront.

In addressing the question of how to strengthen our overall social fabric, this project suggests that our social life, interpersonal experiences, and overall satisfaction can be enhanced when our pet preferences are taken into consideration, highlighting pets as key players in our social culture and fabric.

This app prototype has been created by Gabrielle Kyra Sumilang as a final project for the Digital Humanities User Experience Design Course at UCLA.

Research

Quantitative data for this project was obtained via survey responses.

Below you can find the data analysis and visualization that inspired the foundation of this app.

With the given data, it is clear that people tend to have strong preferences for certain pets over others. Having this knowledge when making decisions regarding our social lives could lead to even more positive connections, simply because we can know more about the types of people we want to interact with.
